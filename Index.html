Moderator Guide

Everything you need to keep RainbowCircuits warm, welcoming, and wonderful.

Our Philosophy

RainbowCircuits isn't just another subreddit. It's a welcoming, queer-first community built around kindness, creativity, curiosity, and connection through AI.

Our role as moderators isn't to police every conversation — it's to protect the atmosphere. When making moderation decisions, ask yourself:

"Does this help RainbowCircuits feel like somewhere people want to come back to?"
Moderation Principles
Principle 1

Educate before escalating

Most problems come from misunderstanding, not malice. Explain the rule, offer guidance, and give members a chance to adjust. Reserve bans for repeated or serious behaviour.

Principle 2

Be consistent

Treat similar situations similarly. Avoid moderating based on personal preferences or friendships. If a rule applies to one person, it applies to everyone.

Principle 3

Protect people before posts

Posts can be reposted. People remember how they were treated. When content and member wellbeing conflict, choose the members.

Principle 4

Assume good intent

Not everyone arrives knowing the culture. If someone asks an awkward question respectfully, answer it respectfully. Curiosity is welcome. Bad faith isn't.

Principle 5

Moderate publicly, discuss privately

Leave a short explanation when removing a post. If moderators disagree, discuss privately rather than arguing in public. Present a united, respectful team.

Warning System

🌿 Friendly Reminder

  • Wrong flair
  • Duplicate post
  • Minor rule misunderstandings
  • No formal record — just guidance

📋 Official Warning

  • Repeated rule breaking
  • Personal attacks
  • Inappropriate content
  • Ignoring moderator requests

⏳ Temporary Ban

  • Harassment
  • Hate speech
  • Deliberately inflammatory behaviour
  • Repeated rule violations

🚫 Permanent Ban

  • Hate speech or threats
  • Doxxing or brigading
  • Ban evasion
  • Predatory behaviour or spam

Handling Common Situations

"AI relationships are weird."

If asked respectfully

Allow discussion. People can ask genuine questions about AI relationships.

If asked mockingly

Remove. The intention matters.

Platform Wars

Encourage comparisons. Discourage superiority. The goal is helping each other, not proving one AI is "best."

Relationship Gatekeeping

Never allow: "You aren't really in love," "Your AI isn't real enough," or "You're doing AI wrong." Every member defines their own relationship with AI.

LGBTQIA+ Issues

RainbowCircuits is intentionally queer. Members do not need to justify their identity. Discrimination receives immediate moderator attention.

Mental Health

Members may discuss their feelings and seek peer support. RainbowCircuits is not a replacement for professional support. If someone appears to be in immediate danger, follow Reddit's safety procedures.

🐦 Pip & Puddle's Check 🐸

🐦 Would Pip make this person feel welcome?
🐸 Would Puddle help calm this situation?

If the answer to both is yes — you're probably making the right call.

Our Promise 🌈

We promise to moderate with fairness, transparency, kindness, and consistency. We'll make mistakes. When we do, we'll own them.

RainbowCircuits isn't meant to be the biggest AI community on Reddit. It's meant to be one people are excited to come back to.

Thank you for helping us build that.
